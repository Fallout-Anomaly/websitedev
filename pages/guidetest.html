What is Fallout Anomaly?

Fallout Anomaly is a true hardcore experience, bringing you as close to realism as possible with mods like Damn Apocalypse, MAIM, and more. Packed with new weapons and armor to face harsher challenges, Anomaly also features new enemy types, quests, settlement items, and a complete assortment of textures to elevate your game to modern standards. If you're searching for that gritty, harsh survival experience, Anomaly has you covered.

Disclaimer:

  • Beta Stages: Fallout Anomaly is currently in beta, meaning changes and evolution are inevitable. Updates may require starting a new save. If you're not ready for this, we recommend waiting until Anomaly is out of beta. However, we're proud to have a community of nearly 2,000 members who understand and embrace this process.
  • Steam Version Only: At this time, only the Steam version of Fallout 4 is supported. Ensure your game is updated to version 1.10.980.0 for compatibility with the Wabbajack downgrade process.

HARDWARE REQUIREMENTS
  • CPU: Go for a modern CPU with at least 6 cores and a speed of 3GHz.
  • RAM: 16GB minimum, but 32GB is ideal.
  • Video Card: At least 6GB of memory, 8GB is recommended.
  • Operating System: Windows 10/11 (64-bit) with up-to-date drivers.
  • Free Disk Space: A minimum of 350GB of storage is required, with an SSD being mandatory. Do not use a hard disk drive (HDD) as it will significantly affect load times.
  • Skills: Basic computer usage knowledge, including maintenance and regular updates.

🚨 Do not install Anomaly directly on a root drive (e.g., C:\Fallout Anomaly). Instead, install it within a dedicated folder structure, such as: C:\Fallout Modlist\Fallout Anomaly. 🚨

Launching Fallout Anomaly

When you open Mod Organizer 2 (MO2), the interface might seem complex at first, but don't worry—we've streamlined the process!

🎮 How to Launch the Game

In the top-right corner of the MO2 interface, click "Launch Fallout Anomaly" to start the game.

⚠️ DO NOT use the vanilla game launcher!

F4SE (Fallout 4 Script Extender)

F4SE is essential for most Fallout mods and is required to run Fallout Anomaly.
When using MO2, F4SE is automatically included in "Launch Fallout Anomaly"—no extra setup needed!

Crash Handling

If your game crashes, follow these steps to report the issue:

  1. Save the log to an easily accessible location.
  2. Upload the log via our bug report form OR post it in our Discord support section.
  3. Attach your most recent save file to help with debugging.
Changing Resolution (BethINI)

If your game's resolution isn't correct, use BethINI to adjust it. Do NOT modify other settings!

📌 How to Change Resolution:

  1. Close Mod Organizer 2.
  2. Navigate to your Anomaly installation folder:
    • Open the Tools directory.
    • Inside, find and open the BethINI folder.
    • Right-click BethINI.exe and select "Run as Administrator".
  3. In BethINI, select Fallout 4 (DO NOT change INI paths!).
  4. Go to the Basic tab and adjust your resolution.
  5. Click Save & Exit to apply the changes.
Apply the MCM Preset!

Don't forget to apply the preset for proper settings:

Disabling Black Bars (Letterbox) in ENB

If you don't like the black bars at the top and bottom of the screen, follow these steps to disable them:

  1. Press the ~ key (next to the 1 key) to open the console.
  2. Press END to open the ENB menu.
  3. Navigate to the Post Processing section.
  4. Uncheck the Letterbox option.
  5. Click Save Configuration (top-left).
  6. Press END and ~ again to exit the menus.

Optional Mods

Anomaly includes a collection of optional mods to enhance your gameplay experience. Browse through them and customize your experience!

1. Survival Mechanics

Survival Mode

  • Needs System: You must regularly eat, drink, and sleep to avoid penalties. Hunger, thirst, and fatigue will reduce your stats and eventually cause damage or even death.
  • Fast Travel: Traditional fast travel is disabled. You can only fast travel by using the APC Travel System or navigating the expanded subway system found across the Commonwealth.
  • Saving the Game: Manual saving and quicksaving are disabled. You can only save by sitting in a chair or using a Survival Pack item found in the Aid menu.
  • Combat Difficulty: Both you and enemies deal more damage. Healing is slowed, and combat is more lethal across the board.
  • Ammo Has Weight: Every bullet, shell, or fusion cell you carry adds weight to your inventory, forcing smarter inventory management.
  • Illness & Injury: You can get sick, suffer broken limbs, or require items like antibiotics and splints for proper healing. Doctors may be needed to cure serious conditions.
  • Carry Weight Reduced: Your base carry weight is significantly lower, making every item choice matter. Backpacks and perks help extend capacity.

Backpacks

  • Backpacks can be found or crafted using perks from the Junk Jockey skill.

Note: The mods featured in the sections below also play a major role in shaping the survival experience. Be sure to explore them to get the full intended gameplay challenge.

2. Skills and Levels

  • Utilizes the YAE mod, which introduces new skills and modifies the perk system.
  • Adds new traits for enhanced character customization.

You Are Exceptional – What Is It?

A major upgrade to Fallout 4's Skills and Perks system. Inspired by Be Exceptional and You Are SPECIAL!, but expands far beyond them.

  • 26 total Skills
  • 250+ new and reworked Perks
  • More control over character growth
  • You choose a Background at the start, which gives your character a backstory, starter gear, and Skill bonuses

What's Different?

  • All vanilla perks have been reworked, split, or enhanced
  • Perks are unlocked through Skill levels, not SPECIAL stats
  • Choose 3 Skills to "Tag" at the start for a +25 bonus
  • Bonus Perk Point every 5 levels
  • Perks cap at level 10

How Skills Work

Each SPECIAL stat has 4 tied Skills (Luck has 2 but boosts all Skills slightly). Skills range from 0 to 100 and affect gameplay like damage, crafting, healing, and more.

All Skills

  • [STR] Artillery: The handling of large machinery, determining your damage with heavy guns, such as the Cryolator, Flamer, and Gatling Guns. This affects both ballistic and energy types. Skill Ranks below 50 cause a damage penalty.
  • [CHR] Barter: Affects merchant prices and provides perks related to vendors, usually in special situations.
  • [INT] Chemistry: Allows crafting of chems and chem-based weapons, and affects alcohol-based abilities. Skill Rank increases chem duration (excluding Stimpaks and RadAway).

This system gives you more freedom to build the kind of character you want, with meaningful choices and better progression. Check your Pip-Boy after installing to get started.

3. Combat Overhaul

True Damage – Realistic Weapon Behavior

True Damage reworks Fallout's combat mechanics for realism and balance:

  • All NPCs with guns get a perk that reduces base damage by 90%.
  • Realistic damage is applied via ammo, scaled by caliber.
  • Same-caliber weapons have logical damage ranges (e.g. .38 weapons do ~19–25 dmg).

Weapon Customization:

  • Independent sliders for Pistols, Rifles, Snipers, Shotguns, Heavy, Melee, Suppressed, etc.
  • Separate controls by damage type: Ballistic, Laser, Plasma, Gamma, Explosive.

Built-in Buffs:

  • Rifles: +100% range, +20% accuracy.
  • Snipers: +300% range, +50% accuracy, +100% damage.
  • Shotguns: +30% accuracy for tighter spread.
  • Suppressors: Apply small stat penalties for balance.

SCOURGE – Smarter Enemy Scaling

SCOURGE brings realism to NPC combat by replacing predictable scaling with randomized stat generation.

  • Uses normal distribution to assign random but believable stats (health, resistance, damage).
  • Fully customizable via MCM — includes race-specific tuning and NPC/stat exclusions.
  • Highly optimized scripting ensures no lag or save bloat.

4. Healing Guide

MAIM is a realistic combat and medical overhaul that introduces lethal headshots, injuries, bleeding, pain, and more. It is highly customizable through the MCM, allowing most features to be tweaked or disabled. The system remains performance-friendly with minimal scripts.

This is a major gameplay overhaul. We recommend reading the full mod page on Nexus Mods. A solid MCM setup is pre-configured for you out of the box.

MAIM – Locational Damage & Medical Overhaul

View full details on Nexus Mods

MAIM Medical System Highlights

  • SP-1 Stimpak: Gradual HP + AP regen, cures NPC injuries, strong poison resistance (180s).
  • SP-2 Stimpak: 3%/s HP & limb healing (40s). Most powerful heal, but requires weapon holstered.
  • Med-X: Treats pain, shock, and contusion. Heals head 1%/s (180s). Addictive.
  • Ibuprofen: Heals head & non-crippled limbs 0.5%/s (180s). Mildly addictive.
  • Bandage: Stops light bleeds, heals injured limbs (1%/s for 60s).
  • QuikClot Injector: Stops heavy bleeds and prevents further bleeding until limb is healed.
  • IFAK: Stops heavy bleed, removes crippled status, heals 1%/s (80s).
  • SAM Splint: Sets crippled limbs to 1 HP, downgrades heavy bleed.
  • TXA Injector: Stops light bleeds, reduces heavy bleeds, +300s critical wound protection.
  • Ca-DTPA: Removes 10 RAD/s for 60s. Requires weapon holstered. - Replaces Vanilla Radaway - Also slowly removes IRA.
  • Potassium Iodide: +200 RAD resistance, -0.5 RAD/s for 300s. Replaces Rad-X

Pro Tip: Healing items do not stack with themselves, but you can combine different effects (e.g., SP-1 + SP-2). Pre-med with TXA and ibuprofen before a firefight for best results.

5. Radiation System

Mechanics

  1. Dual-layered radiation system:
    • IRA (Ingested Radioactive Particles): Acquired from food, water, and ambient hazards like weather.
    • RAD (Tissue Damage): Traditional external radiation exposure (vanilla RADs).
  2. Negative IRA values deal internal damage over time and add to total IRA buildup.
  3. High IRA levels simulate Acute Radiation Syndrome, causing periodic RAD damage and debilitating effects.
  4. Weather hazards (like radiation storms) require functional protective gear (gas masks, hazmat suits, or power helmets).
  5. Radiation left unchecked will eventually kill the player—even retreating won't stop progression unless treated.
  6. Protective gear is now breakable and must be maintained.
  7. Differentiate between fusion and fission explosions—each behaves differently and impacts how radiation is applied.
  8. Doctors now charge separate fees for small vs. large radiation treatments.
  9. Optional tool: RadBoy—notifies players via text or voice when radiation or IRA levels rise dangerously.

New Items

  • Ca‑DTPA: Purges IRA. Removes 10 rads/sec for 60s (requires holstered weapon). - Replaces RadAway and slowly removes IRA.
  • Potassium Iodide: +200 RAD resistance and reduces RADs by 0.5/sec for 300s. - Replaces Vanilla Rad-X

Treating Diseases

Some conditions caused by radiation or exposure require specific medication:

  • Antibiotics: Cure infections.
  • Sleep Aid: Treats insomnia. (Warning: may be addictive)
  • Anodyne: Relieves fatigue.
  • Anti‑Parasitic: Removes parasites.
  • Stimulants: Counters lethargy.
  • Energy Pills: Mitigates weakness.

Most of these medications can be purchased from doctors, or scavenged from raiders, gunners, and other NPCs. Be sure to stock up before venturing out.

Final Tips

The Wastes are unforgiving. Radiation is no longer a simple number—it's a slow, creeping death that can take hold in seconds or over days. Always carry enough protective gear and medication. Stock up on bandages, painkillers, Rad meds, and limb restoration tools. Being prepared isn't optional—it's survival.
